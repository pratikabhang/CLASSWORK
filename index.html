    Note: This 10-point grading system is commonly used in Indian universities and many other institutions worldwide. The exact percentage ranges may vary slightly between universities.

    Formulas

    Percentage Calculation:

    Percentage = (Obtained Marks / Total Marks) × 100

    GPA Calculation (4.0 scale):

    GPA = Sum of (Grade Points × Credit Hours) / Total Credit Hours

    Example: If you got A (4.0) in a 3-credit course and B (3.0) in a 4-credit course:
    GPA = (4.0 × 3 + 3.0 × 4) / (3 + 4) = (12 + 12) / 7 = 24 / 7 ≈ 3.43

    SGPA Calculation (10.0 scale):

    SGPA = Sum of (Grade Points × Credits) / Total Credits

    Example: If you got 9 in a 4-credit course and 7 in a 3-credit course:
    SGPA = (9 × 4 + 7 × 3) / (4 + 3) = (36 + 21) / 7 = 57 / 7 ≈ 8.14

    CGPA Calculation (10.0 scale):

    CGPA = Sum of (SGPA × Semester Credits) / Total Credits Across All Semesters

    Example: Semester 1 SGPA 8.5 (20 credits), Semester 2 SGPA 7.8 (22 credits):
    CGPA = (8.5 × 20 + 7.8 × 22) / (20 + 22) = (170 + 171.6) / 42 ≈ 8.13

    Important Notes:

    • Some universities calculate CGPA as a simple average of SGPAs rather than credit-weighted
    • Always check your institution's specific grading policy
    • Conversion between different grading systems is approximate
    • This conversion is based on SPPU 2019 rulebook and Circular No. 322/2020
